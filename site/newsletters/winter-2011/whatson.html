What's on in London this Winter

Theatre

  • Cirque du Soleil-Royal Albert Hall - 5 January - 17 February
  • Salad Days-Riverside Studios Until - 6 February In one of the happiest and best-loved classic musicals ever, Timothy and Jane leave university to enter an enchanted, sunny, happy 1954 where a street piano makes everyone dance, uncorks our inner glee and turns all of our lives around for the better.
  • Twelfth Night-Cottesloe, National Theatre - 11 January - 2 March Directed by Sir Peter Hall and starring his daughter Rebecca and Simon Callow
  • The Children's Hour-Comedy Theatre - 22 January - 2 April Lillian Hellman's 1934 drama about two headmistresses of a New England boarding school. Starring Kiera Knightly and Elisabeth Moss.
  • Frankenstien-Olivier, National Theatre - 5 February - 17 April Directed by Danny Boyle starring Benedict Cumberbach and Jonny Lee Miller in Nick Dear's new adaptation.
  • The Wizard of Oz-London Palladium - From 7th February The latest Andrew Lloyd Webber production
  • Cause Célèbre-The Old Vic - 17 March - 11 June Based on the true story of Alma Rattenbury who went on trial with her 18-year-old lover for the murder of her husband, Cause Célèbre is an intriguing tale of love, betrayal, guilt and obsession.
